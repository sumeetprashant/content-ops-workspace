## The 3 Pressure Points

### 1. "Your Students Hear the Difference"

The #1 complaint on eMedicoz reviews is audio quality. Medical students consume lectures at 1.5x-2x speed. At that speed, every audio imperfection is amplified. Background hum becomes a headache. Room echo becomes unbearable.

**Our studio has 4-layer acoustic isolation built to music recording standards.** Not podcast-grade. Not office-quiet. MUSIC GRADE. Rock, wood, carpet, foam. The kind of silence where you can hear a pin drop on the other side of the room.

When a student hits 2x speed on a lecture recorded in our studio, they hear crystal clear voice. When they hit 2x on current DAMS content, they hear noise.

**This is not marketing. This is physics.**

### 2. "Marrow Already Knows This"

We produce medical education video content for platforms in the NEET/USMLE space. We don't need to say "Marrow" by name. Just: "platforms in your space." Let Dr. Sethi connect the dots himself.

When he realizes the studio behind his competitor's polished content is 30 minutes from his office -- and is available to him too -- the urgency becomes personal.

**We don't say:** "We work with Marrow"
**We say:** "We produce medical education video for platforms you compete with. The quality difference starts in the studio."

Let him ask "which platforms?" Let HIM pursue that thread. The moment he's asking us questions, we've won the frame.

### 3. "NExT Changes Everything"

NExT (National Exit Test) is replacing/supplementing NEET PG. DAMS is pivoting hard -- their app is already rebranded. This means:
- Hundreds of hours of NEW content needed
- Old content can't just be recycled
- This is a once-in-a-decade reset moment

**If DAMS records NExT content with the same setup they used for NEET PG, they'll fall further behind.** But if they record NExT content in a professional studio, they leapfrog everyone.

Timing is everything. NExT is the window.

## The Pitch Sequence

### Step 1: LinkedIn (Plant the Seed)

Don't sell. Create curiosity.

> Dr. Sethi -- I run a production studio in Sector 65 that specializes in medical education video. With NExT content production ramping up, thought you might want to see what 4-layer acoustic isolation does for lecture quality at 2x playback. We're 30 min from Green Park -- open for a quick studio visit anytime.

**Why this works:**
- "Medical education video" -- not generic "video production"
- "4-layer acoustic isolation" -- technical, specific, impressive
- "2x playback" -- shows we understand HIS students' behavior
- "30 min from Green Park" -- we know where DAMS is. We did our homework
- "Studio visit" -- low commitment. Curiosity-driven

### Step 2: The Studio Visit (Close the Frame)

When he walks in, the studio sells itself. But we control the narrative:

**First 30 seconds:** Record his voice on his phone in our lobby (normal room). Then record the same sentence inside the studio. Play both back at 2x speed. The difference is undeniable.

**Next 5 minutes:** Show him the 4-layer wall cross-section. Rock. Wood. Carpet. Foam. Explain why music recording standards matter for medical education. "Your students don't just listen. They STUDY. For hours. At 2x. Every imperfection compounds."

**Next 5 minutes:** Show him a before/after. Take a sample DAMS lecture from eMedicoz (publicly available). Run it through CS Labs AI audio enhancement. Play the enhanced version. Then say: "This is what we can do to your EXISTING library without re-recording anything. And this is what NEW recordings sound like from day one."

**The kill shot:** "We've set up for a quick demo. Sit in that chair. Let's record 3 minutes of you talking about any topic. Watch the playback. That's what your students would see."

Once he sees HIMSELF in professional multi-camera, with perfect audio, perfect lighting -- he can't unsee it. The gap between that and his current eMedicoz content becomes visceral.

### Step 3: The Proposal (Make It Easy to Say Yes)

Don't propose a massive contract. Propose something so small and so obviously valuable that saying no feels stupid.

**"The NExT Pilot"**
- 1 day in our studio
- 5 hero lectures (your choice of subject -- Radiology?)
- Multi-camera professional production
- Full post-production: editing, color grading, audio mastering, branded overlays
- Delivered in eMedicoz-ready format
- **INR 1.5-2 lakh total** (position as "pilot pricing")

**Why pilot works:**
- Low risk for Dr. Sethi (he's bootstrapped, he hates waste)
- Creates undeniable A/B comparison material
- Once 5 lectures look amazing and the rest look dated, the rest of the library becomes an embarrassment he needs to fix
- The pilot IS the sales tool for the full contract

### Step 4: The Upsell (After the Pilot)

Once the pilot is done:

**Immediate upsell:** "Your existing library has 100+ hours of content. Students are consuming it right now with subpar audio. We can run your entire library through CS Labs AI audio enhancement. INR 8,000/hr -- but for the full library we'll do a package rate."

**Long-term upsell:** "NExT needs hundreds of hours of new content. We can set up a regular recording schedule -- 2 days/month in our studio. Your faculty flies through their subjects. We handle everything else."
