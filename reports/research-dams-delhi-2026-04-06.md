## 1. Content Strategy

### Current Content Output

DAMS operates a multi-format content ecosystem centered on their **eMedicoz** digital platform (1M+ downloads, 500K+ registered doctors and students):

- **Video Lectures:** Extensive recorded video library covering all NEET PG, NExT, FMGE, USMLE subjects. Videos are the core product -- students purchase "Videos Only" courses on eMedicoz.
- **LIVE Interactive Classes:** Two-way interactive teaching sessions (India's first for NEET PG, per their claim). Students can ask questions in real time.
- **Social Media:** Active Instagram presence (229K followers, 20K+ posts via @dams_delhi). YouTube presence exists but is not a primary distribution channel.
- **App-Based Distribution:** eMedicoz app is the primary delivery vehicle, available on Android and iOS. Features include video lectures, question banks with video solutions, clinical case feeds, and a social network for doctors.

### Production Model

DAMS appears to produce content **primarily in-house** with their faculty recording lectures. There is no evidence of a dedicated external production partner or professional studio setup. The content is faculty-driven -- Dr. Sumer Sethi (founder) and subject-matter experts record lectures that are then distributed through eMedicoz.

### Quality Level

**Mid-tier at best.** User reviews consistently highlight:
- Poor audio quality in some videos
- App UX issues (crashes, broken video solutions, confusing navigation)
- Users describe feeling "a severe letdown" compared to Marrow when it comes to production value
- The teaching content itself is well-regarded, but the production wrapper is weak

### Publishing Frequency

High volume -- DAMS covers all major medical subjects from 1st year MBBS through dedicated NEET PG courses, plus resident doctor and CME content. LIVE classes run regularly. However, the refresh rate and modernization of older recorded content is unclear.
